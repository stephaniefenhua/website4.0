Superposition IV is a 24-hour Bay Area hackathon for female-identifying (trans and non-binary inclusive) high school and college students of all experience levels and backgrounds. This event is free to attend, and wi-fi, food, swag, and workspaces will be provided. Bring your passion for technology and we'll take care of the rest! Any female-identifying high school or college student is welcome to attend. Although we do not accept males as attendees, we wholeheartedly welcome all genders to sign up as a mentor, workshop leader, judge, or volunteer!

Frequently Asked Questions

A hackathon is a 24-hour event where students develop solutions to real-world problems using technology. Provided with free food, mentors, and awesome activities, students often work on a personal project to pitch at the end of the hackathon. In addition to huge prizes, students can walk away with new friendships and an even greater love of coding. Overall, hackathons are here to provide a supportive environment for you to learn and explore your interests.

This event is absolutely free to attend. Get ready for free swag, food, and lots of fun!
